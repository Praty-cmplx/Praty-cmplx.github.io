Research Interests

I have worked on a variety of problems over the last four years - effects of community structure on synchronization and opinion polarization; how heterogeneities in populations can change the rumour spreading dynamics in Maki-Thompson model; phase space analysis of finite Kuramoto systems. Broadly, I am interested in studying complex systems - systems where two and two put together do not make four! these could be systems ranging from a collection of neurons to a collection of people. While my interest in this subject started out as purely academic, I have realized that a lot of ongoing research in this field has a huge implications for the society. I want to work on such socially relevant problems, with my end goal being :

  • Understanding how humans cooperate, compete and interact over a range of scales - local (families or friends at school) to global (the internet).
  • To figure out the best ways to organize human societies to achieve optimal liberty, equality and dignity.

Apart from these, I am also interested in the more theoretical aspects of complex systems. Complex systems are everywhere - from societies, the human body to the earth and climate itself. To answer the fundamental questions of defining complexity and figuring out a general theory of complex systems, I want to explore the wide range of aforementioned systems too. While I do not have any experience at this front, I hope to explore it in future.
